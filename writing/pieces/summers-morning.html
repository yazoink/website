The sun beat down with a nauseating heaviness. The shadow cast by the end of the rail lay like a cat's tail against the white concrete -- a chalky shore to the bright sea of grass.


The washing line stood precariously like a rickety old lighthouse; single metallic leg quivering like an abused street sign. It looked ready to sink into the scratchy green ocean and drown in eternal rest. A weary towel rested apologetically on its head.
