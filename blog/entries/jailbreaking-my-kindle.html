I bought a secondhand 7th gen Kindle recently and decided to jailbreak it because I hate Amazon and feel that it is my duty to do the equivalent of installing Linux on every device I can get my hands on. My jailbreak involved installing KUAL and KOReader with the help of WinterBreak. You may be wondering what reasons there are for doing this outside the standard freetard spiel--I was pretty unsure, going into it, but this is what I found:


  • Calibre integration
  • SSH capability
  • Better ability to sideload books (via USB transfer, SFTP, or Calibre)
  • Compatibility with way more file types (MOBI, CBZ, etc.)
  • A million more customisation options in KOReader as opposed to the default Kindle reader
  • KOReader plugins and patches
  • Custom screensavers
  • In-depth reading statistics

I'm working with a 7th gen Kindle Paperwhite. You can use this article as a tutorial is you have a the same or a similar model, but it won't the cover anything outside the scope of what I did. Check here to see which methods are compatible with which model. If you own a 7th gen Paperwhite and are considering doing this, make sure you are not on firmware 5.18.1 or beyond.


My starting place and primary source was the Kindle Modding Wiki. Some of the resources are outdated, but it generally serves as a great guide, along with this video.


  1. To start, I downloaded WinterBreak to my laptop (here) and extracted it. Then I put my Kindle into airplane mode and rebooted.
  2. Once booted, I plugged it into my laptop and copied the extracted files (there is a hidden file: .active_content_sandbox. make sure not to miss it). Once this was complete I ejected the Kindle from the computer.
  3. On the Kindle, I opened the store and disabled airplane mode. It opened into Mesquito and from there I launched WinterBreak (at this stage, delete update.bin.partial from the Kindle if present to prevent an automatic update). Wait until it says something along the lines of "Now you are ready to install the hotfix".
  4. The hotfix ensures that the jailbreak will persist after updating. It can be downloaded here. I plugged the Kindle into the laptop again and copied Update_hotfix_universal.bin to it, then ejected it. Clicked on the three dots in the corner if the screen and selected Update Your Kindle. Once the hotfix had finished installing I ran the Run Hotfix booklet to install it.
  5. Once this was done I plugged the Kindle back in to install the MPRI package manager and KUAL launcher. (here under KUAL & KUAL extensions; MR Package Installer for MRPI and KUAL (coplate) for KUAL). Extracted the MRPI archive and copied extensions/ and mrpackages/ to Kindle. Extracted KUAL archive and copied Update_KUALBooklet_*_install.bin to Kindle. Updated kindle via three-dot menu again. Once finished and rebooted, KUAL is installed as a booklet.
  6. To prevent OTA updates (which break the jailbreak) I downloaded the RenameOTA KUAL extension and copied the extracted ZIP to the extensions/ directory on the Kindle. (this process is different on firmware <=5.10.x, see Kindle Modding Wiki). Opened KUAL and selected Rename OTA Binaries and Rename.
  7. Once the Kindle had rebooted I downloaded the kindlehf KOReader package to my laptop from here. Extracted package and copied files to Kindle.

With KOReader installed, it can be launched from KUAL. I end up installing various plugins and patches to KOReader, my favourite being ProjectTitle (a complete UI overhaul). You can find more on Github and MobileRead.
